The SodaFier

During HackMCST, I worked with a group of my friends to create the SodaFier. The SodaFier is an up-and-coming soda company that has a variety of delicious flavors.

Flavors include Cola, Cream Soda, Ginger Ale, Grape, Oranagade, and Zesty Lime.

As for the inner workings of the SodaFier, it included a Raspberry Pi, motors, and a bunch of wiring. The outside of the SodaFier had an LCD screen that would change text based on whether there was a cup under the soda dispensing position. Additionally, it had a button to start and stop soda dispensing.

My group and I started with an idea and manifested it into reality and we even placed on the podium at HackMCST 2024. This project has taught me to trust in the knowledge I've learned over the years and have fun.
Haunted House CYOA

I created the Haunted House CYOA with my good friend Nicolas. Originally, we planned on creating a simple console-based program but it soon evolved into a semi-game with a custom GUI.

The main objective is to get out of the Haunted House. To do that, the player must collect equipment and defeat the boss. If the player does not collect enough equipment, the boss will defeat the player. The player gets pieces of equipment by killing weak mods or if the player is lucky will randomly receive one.

The thought behind this game was to create a Haunted House where you could choose your own adventure. There are countless outcomes and many different ways to win.

Creating this game inspired me to continue my computer science journey, and it allowed me to learn and improve my coding skills.
Caesar Cipher Encryption and Decryption Tool

Encryption is essential for securing sensitive information and preventing unauthorized access.

My CaesarCipher Encryption and Decryption Tool lets people to encrypt important information with a key, preventing unwanted information leaks. When the user wants to read the information again, the user can use the decryption tool to decrypt the information with the key.

My Caesar cipher also includes encryption and decryption of numbers that normal Caesar ciphers do not have.

This project helped me step into the world of cybersecurity and broadened my understanding of the topic. I hope to continue to improve my skills through meaningful projects like this.
